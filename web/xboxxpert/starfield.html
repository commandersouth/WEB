Starfield Review: A Flawed Masterpiece

(Review text powered by Bing AI Chat, modified by reviewer)

Starfield is a sci-fi RPG that takes you on an epic journey across the galaxy. You can create your own character, customize your spaceship, and explore more than 1000 planets with different environments, NPCs, and mysteries. The game promises to deliver a rich and immersive experience that will let you shape your own story.

The graphics of Starfield are impressive, but not breathtaking. The game uses a realistic style that makes the planets look detailed and diverse, but also somewhat bland and lifeless. The character models are well-done, though they manage to have glitches now and again. The animations are smooth and fluid. The lighting and shadows are realistic, while nothing is as breathtaking as the first time we saw soft shadows in Oblivion on 360..

The audio and music of Starfield are excellent. The game features a dynamic soundtrack that adapts to your actions and surroundings, creating a sense of tension and excitement. The voice acting is superb, with talented actors bringing the characters to life. The sound effects are crisp and immersive, making you feel like you are in space.

The gameplay of Starfield is fun and engaging, though after a while you start to see the same buildings and colony configurations. I did so VERY quickly and it hurt the immersion. The game offers a lot of freedom and choice in how you approach each quest, and to be sure there are a multitude of quests to go through. I played for 60 hours on my first play through and I feel like I only did 2 main quest lines. And I mostly ignored the radiant quests, though I did to a few.

The story of Starfield is amazing. The game has a compelling plot that revolves around the mystery of the origin of humanity in space. You join Constellation, a group of space explorers seeking rare artifacts throughout the galaxy. You encounter various factions, aliens, dangers, and secrets along the way. You make choices that affect the outcome of the story. You experience twists and turns that keep you hooked. Which brings me into how it ties together.

The new game plus mode of Starfield is awesome. It allows you to continue your adventure with all your progress intact. Furthermore, it is implemented as a function of the story which was so different than how you normally see it happen. You can revisit any planet or quest you want with new challenges or rewards. You can also unlock new features or items as you level up. Beyond that, each play through can have things from previous playthroughs impact the next run (though I have not seen a MAJOR change at all). It's truly a unique implementation of the mechanic and really excites me about the game.

Overall, I think Starfield is a great game that delivers on its promises. It has stunning graphics that create a realistic space setting, amazing audio that enhances the mood, fun gameplay that offers variety, an amazing story that captivates you, and to beat all that, an awesome new game plus mode that adds replay value. I give it a 9 out of 10 stars.

While it doesn't reach the heights it may have intended, it nails enough to be a memorable experience like no other.

Final Score: 9/10
